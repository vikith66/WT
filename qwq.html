World Wide Web Consortium (W3C)

About W3C

The World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) is an international community that develops open standards to ensure the long-term growth of the Web. W3C's mission is to lead the Web to its full potential by developing protocols and guidelines that ensure the Web's smooth operation and evolution.

W3C Mission

The W3C's mission is to develop web standards that ensure the Web's longevity and growth. It focuses on several key areas such as accessibility, internationalization, and open web standards.

History of W3C

The W3C was founded by Sir Tim Berners-Lee, the inventor of the World Wide Web, in 1994. Since its inception, the W3C has played a crucial role in the development of web standards and guidelines that shape the modern Web.
